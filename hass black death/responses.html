Black Death

Religious Responses

People believed that the Black Death was a punishment from God which they saw as a divine retribution for human sins. The public prayer became more common, where a group of people would gather at a religious place like a temple or a mosque and beg for mercy and seek forgiveness. Penitential procession became a widespread response to overwhelm death and suffering.
